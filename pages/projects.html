Distributed Energy Resources System Impact Study

Expecting an influx of solar power developers seeking interconnection to the grid, Portland General Electric needed to understand the impacts to its distribution system.

To study how multiple solar interconnections would affect the utility’s 12.47 kV distribution system, PGE and POWER first used CYME to model feeder SCADA and advanced metering infrastructure (AMI) data. This verified that the feeder model operated as expected under different loading conditions.

Then we added the interconnect to the model. Using various loading scenarios, the study evaluated the system’s response and the effects on feeder loading, voltage, protection and sub-transmission or transmission systems.

The research helped PGE navigate the growth of distributed energy resources (DER) on its system.

Great Northern 500 kV Transmission Line Substation Design Studies

To successfully deliver 883 megawatts of Canadian hydropower across 224 miles of new 500 kV transmission line, Minnesota Power needed to make sure its substations were ready to handle the new capacity.

The 224-mile-long, 500 kV Great Northern Transmission Line (GNTL) connects with Manitoba Hydro across the U.S.-Canadian border. The project includes the new 500 kV/230 kV Iron Range substation and modifications to the existing Blackberry 500 kV/230 kV substation.

Minnesota Power engaged POWER Engineers for owner’s engineering, detailed transmission and substation design, and program and construction support services. As part of POWER’s substation design services, we provided electrical studies that helped Minnesota Power prepare for and mitigate the risks of connecting so much new power to its grid.

When finished in 2020, the line will allow Minnesota Power to diversify its energy sources, meet increasing demand and supplement system reliability.

Harry Allen-Eldorado 500 kV Transmission Project

DesertLink built a 60-mile-long, 500 kV transmission line to provide California’s power grid new access to renewable generation.

The project in southern Nevada also was planned to relieve congestion and improve the region’s available transfer capability. This high-profile project crosses near the suburban Las Vegas area as well as environmentally sensitive desert terrain in southern Nevada.

POWER provided support to DesertLink, a subsidiary of LS Power, in its bid to the California Independent System Operator for this project. After CAISO awarded the project to DesertLink, POWER became the engineer of record, providing design engineering and the necessary transmission design studies to ensure the line’s safe and efficient operation.
